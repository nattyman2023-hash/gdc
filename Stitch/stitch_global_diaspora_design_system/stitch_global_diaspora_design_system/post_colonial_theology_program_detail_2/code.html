Curriculum Architecture

A rigorous intellectual journey spanning four semesters of transformative study.

01

Foundations of Post-Colonial Thought

Core Requirement • Semester 1

expand_more

An intensive investigation into the historical interplay between European colonialism and Christian expansion. Students will critique traditional mission paradigms through the lens of Said, Fanon, and modern Global South theologians.

  • check_circle Epistemic Justice & Theological Method
  • check_circle History of the 'Imperial Christ'
  • check_circle Decolonial Reading of Sacred Texts
02

Diaspora Ecclesiology

Specialized Elective • Semester 2

expand_more

Exploring the unique theological contributions of migrant communities. This module addresses how displaced people-groups reshape church identity and mission strategy in the 21st century.

  • check_circle Theologies of Migration & Hospitality
  • check_circle Reverse Mission Dynamics
03

Reconciliation & Social Restitution

Applied Mission • Semester 3

expand_more

Beyond peacebuilding: a rigorous study of restitution as a biblical mandate. Students will design practical frameworks for local church engagement in systemic justice.

  • check_circle Reparative Justice Models
  • check_circle Community-Based Mission Action

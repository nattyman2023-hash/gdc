Admissions Process

A Legacy of Excellence

At Global Diaspora University, we seek students who are not only academically gifted but also driven by a sense of divine purpose. Our admissions process is designed to identify individuals who will contribute to our community of faith and scholarship.

"For I know the plans I have for you," declares the Lord, "plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future."

Entry Requirements

  • check_circle Official high school transcripts or equivalent with a minimum 3.5 GPA.
  • check_circle Statement of Faith and Personal Testimony.
  • check_circle Two letters of recommendation (one academic, one pastoral/spiritual).
  • check_circle Standardized test scores (SAT or ACT preferred, but optional for international diaspora applicants).
  • check_circle Proof of English proficiency for non-native speakers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the application deadline?

Early action deadline is November 15th. Regular decision applications are accepted until February 1st.

Are scholarships available?

Yes, over 85% of our diaspora students receive some form of merit-based or mission-focused financial aid.

Can I transfer credits?

GDU accepts transfer credits from accredited institutions globally, subject to a formal transcript evaluation.

Do you offer online programs?

We offer a hybrid learning model designed to accommodate students living across different time zones.
